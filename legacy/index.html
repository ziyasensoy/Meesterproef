The North Sea

When you look at the North Sea, you see water.

But beneath the surface lies another world.

You break through the surface.

The light shifts. The world goes quiet.

01
01 / Surface

Above the surface, the North Sea is one of Europe's busiest regions.

International Shipping

Over 400,000 ships cross these waters each year, carrying goods between continents and connecting global trade routes.

Oil & Gas

For decades, offshore platforms have extracted fossil fuels from beneath the seabed, shaping the region's economy.

Offshore Wind Energy

The North Sea hosts some of the world's largest wind farms, generating clean electricity for millions of European homes.

02 / Life Below

A living ecosystem thrives beneath the waves.

Click the creatures - each one holds a story.

03
03 / Traces

Human activity leaves traces beneath the surface.

Plastic Pollution

Every year, thousands of tonnes of plastic enter the North Sea - from bottles to microplastics invisible to the eye.

Ghost Nets

Lost fishing gear continues to trap marine life long after it falls overboard, drifting silently through the water.

Noise Pollution

Ship engines and construction noise disrupt the communication and navigation of whales, dolphins, and other species.

04
04 / Deep Network

Deep beneath the waves lies an invisible infrastructure connecting countries and powering millions of homes.

Internet Cables

Subsea fibre-optic cables carry over 99% of international data traffic between Europe and the world.

Power Cables

High-voltage lines link offshore wind farms to national grids, delivering renewable energy ashore.

Gas Pipelines

An extensive network of pipelines transports natural gas between nations beneath the seabed.

05 / Borders & Power

06 / Connection

Everything in the North Sea is connected.

Ships carry goods that sustain economies across the continent.

Wind farms and pipelines power homes from Scotland to the Netherlands.

Seals, fish, and seabirds form a web of life that spans national borders.

Cables and pipelines link nations in ways most people never see.

The North Sea is more than water.

A source of energy.

A route for trade.

A home for wildlife.
